Review of Here To Stay by Mark Edwards

Abstract

Here to Stay is about a happy, successful young man who purchased and renovated a beautiful old home in an upper middle class neighborhood just outside London. Well, he was happy until the Robinson’s moved in.

When Elliot met Gemma Robinson his world was turned upside down. It became a whirlwind romance and within weeks they were married and living in the home that Elliot considered his pride and joy.

Gemma’s parents and sister came to stay with them shortly after they were married, for what Gemma said would be a couple of weeks, and that’s when the never-ending torment begins.

If you’ve ever wondered what it would be like to live with your in-laws, it’s nothing compared to what Elliot and Gemma face in this scary novel. We’re not just talking privacy, kitchen privileges and bathroom sharing issues here. This is your worst nightmare!

True Happiness?

This quote by Elliot explains his reasoning for getting married. This would normally be way out of his “reserved Englishman” comfort zone. Listen, I know you’re going to say it’s too quick. That’s what everyone is going to say. But I’m not going to change my mind. You know when I was stung by the bee? I made a vow to myself then that I wasn’t going to waste time anymore. I wasn’t going to allow fear or doubt to rule me. She makes me happy, Amira. So happy.
